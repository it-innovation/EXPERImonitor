About

Social networking technology provides major new opportunities for policy makers (eGovernment) to engage with the community (eSociety), and this website is the second prototype of a toolset that allows policy makers (e.g. MPs, representatives of local and national governments) to take full advantage to be taken of a wide range of existing and well established social networking sites in order to engage citizens in two-way dialogs as part of the governance and policymaking processes.

A key feature of our approach is to allow policy makers to move away from the limitations inherent in the current practice of using bespoke and dedicated platforms (e.g. specific opinion soliciting websites hosted by governments) and instead make full use of the high levels of participation and rich discussions that already take place in existing social networking communities. In short, use the platforms the citizens use already.

WeGov is developing the tools and techniques for closing the loop between policy makers and the citizens, and this website contains a number of configurable tools to perform tasks such as searching on social networking sites and analysis of the results. The tools are being added to all the time, and make it possible to detect, track and mine opinions and discussions on policy oriented topics.

For more information please visit: http://www.wegov-project.eu/
